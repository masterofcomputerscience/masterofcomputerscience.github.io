A-Level Computer Science 9618

Master the Art of Computational Thinking

Take your passion for technology to the next level. Cambridge A-Level Computer Science (9618) is a rigorous, intellectually demanding course designed for students who don't just want to use technology — they want to understand, build, and innovate with it.

From the elegant logic of algorithms to the architecture powering modern processors, this course challenges you to think like a computer scientist. You'll explore the theoretical foundations that drive every line of code, every network packet, and every intelligent system in the world around you.

What You'll Master:

Dive deep into data structures, programming paradigms, computer architecture, networking, databases, artificial intelligence, and the mathematical principles that underpin it all. Whether you're writing recursive functions or designing relational databases, every concept connects theory to the real world.

Assessment Structure — 4 Papers:

🖥️ Paper 1 — Theory Fundamentals (Written Exam — 3 hours)

A deep dive into core computer science concepts — from hardware and software to data representation, communication, and security.

💡 Paper 2 — Fundamental Problem-Solving & Programming (Written Exam — 2 hours)

Put your algorithmic thinking to the test. Design solutions, trace programs, and demonstrate your programming knowledge on paper.

⌨️ Paper 3 — Advanced Theory (Written Exam — 1.5 hours)

Explore cutting-edge topics including artificial intelligence, machine learning, computational thinking, and systems software.

💻 Paper 4 — Practical Programming (Practical Exam — 2.5 hours)

The ultimate test. Sit at a real computer and solve problems using your programming language of choice — Python, Java, or another approved language. This is where theory meets execution.

Why Choose A-Level Computer Science?

This qualification is your gateway to top universities and careers in software engineering, cybersecurity, data science, artificial intelligence, and beyond. It's not just a subject — it's the language of the future.
