A-Level Computer Science 9618. Master the Art of Computational Thinking

📘 Course Overview

Take your passion for technology to the next level. Cambridge A-Level Computer Science (9618) is a rigorous, intellectually demanding course designed for students who don't just want to use technology — they want to understand, build, and innovate with it.

From the elegant logic of algorithms to the architecture powering modern processors, this course challenges you to think like a computer scientist. You'll explore the theoretical foundations that drive every line of code, every network packet, and every intelligent system.

What You'll Master

Data structures, programming paradigms, computer architecture, networking, databases, artificial intelligence, and mathematical foundations — all connected to real-world applications.

📝 Assessment Structure

  • Paper 1: Theory Fundamentals
  • Paper 2: Fundamental Problem-solving and Programming Skills
  • Paper 3: Advanced Theory
  • Paper 4: Practical
